Cell Structure and Division

Eukaryotic Cells

Animal, plant, algal and fungal cells are all eukayotic. Organelles' structures determine and assist their functions within the cell. The highly compartmentalised nature of the cell makes it efficient. Cell organelles in eukaryotes include the cell-surface membrane, nucleus, mitochondrion, chloroplast, Golgi apparatus, Golgi vesicle, lysosomes, ribosomes, endoplasmic reitculums, vacuole and cell wall.

Cell Analysis

Cell fractionation is a process in which cells are broken up and different organelles are separated out. Its steps include homogenation and ultracentrifugation. During homogenation, sample is put into a homogeniser and the cell debris is then cleared up and filtered. During ultracentrifugation, the filtrate is placed into a centrifuge and spun at a low speed. This forms a thin pellet at the bottom- the nuclues. The supernatant is then transferred to another test tube, put into the centrifuge and the steps are repeated at higher speeds.
