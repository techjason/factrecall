GCSE Biology

Cell Structure

Cells are basic units for all forms of life. There are two types of cells you need to know: eukaryotic cells and prokaryotic cells. Eukaryotes include animals, plants, algae and fungi. Prokaryotes include bacteria and archaea. Cells carry out essential functions for life - many metabolic and biological reactions occur including respiration, photosynthesis, protein syntheis and so on.

Microbes and Disease

Pathogens are microorganisms that enter the body and cause disease. Communicable diseases include bacterial, viral, protist and fungal:

  • Bacterial diseases make you feel ill by producing toxins that damage your cells and tissues, such diseases include salmonella and gonorrhoea.
  • Viral diseases make people feel ill by causing cell damage when they reproduce, such diseases include measles, HIV, tobacco mosaic virus and the corona virus.
  • Fungal diseases include rose black spot, which is a fungus that causes purple of black spots to develop on the leaves of rose plants.
  • Protist diseases include malaria, in wchih part of the malarial protist's life cycle takes place inside the mosquito.
